### **2. Visual Identity (The "Look")**

#### **Color Palette: "Dark Mode Efficiency"**

Your users are likely sitting in front of screens all day. Go for a dark-themed UI that feels premium and reduces eye strain.

* **Primary Background:** `Deep Charcoal` or `Midnight Blue` (e.g., Hex: `#0F172A`). Avoid pure black; it’s too harsh.
* **Primary Accent (The "Action" Color):** `Electric Indigo` or `Laser Blue` (e.g., Hex: `#4F46E5`). Use this for the "Generate" button and active states.
* **Secondary Accent:** `Neon Mint` or `Cyan` (e.g., Hex: `#22D3EE`). Use this for success states or highlighting AI elements.
* **Text:** `Off-White` / `Grey-200` for high readability.

#### **Typography: "Systematic & Clean"**

Do not use decorative fonts. The font should disappear so the content stands out.

* **Primary Font:** **Inter** or **Geist Sans** (The Vercel font). It’s variable, highly legible at small sizes, and screams "Software Engineering."
* **Headlines:** **Space Grotesk** (optional). If you want a slight "tech" edge for headers on the landing page.

### **6. UI Aesthetic (The "Vibe" of the App)**

Since you are building a tool that generates visuals, the UI itself must be invisible.

* **Glassmorphism:** Use subtle glass effects on the control panels (blur backgrounds) to make the generated image feel like it's "under a lens."
* **Grid Lines:** Show subtle guide lines or grid dots in the background of the editor workspace. It makes the user feel like they have precision tools.
* **Instant Feedback:** The brand promise is speed. Use loading bars that say "Analyzing text..." or "Composing layout..." instead of a spinning wheel. It makes the AI feel tangible.
